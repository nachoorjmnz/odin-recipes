How to do Migas

Migas is a traditional Spanish dish with humble origins that has become a beloved staple in Spanish cuisine. The word "migas" translates to "crumbs" in English, and the dish is essentially a clever way of using stale bread to create a hearty and flavorful meal.

  • bread crumbs
  • spanish chorizo
  • bacon
  • olive oil
  • garlic
  • green pepper
  • red pepper
  • eggs
  • water
  • salt

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  2. Add the bread crumbs and stir to coat in the olive oil. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the chorizo and bacon to the pan and cook for 5-7 minutes or until browned.
  4. Add the peppers and cook for 3-4 minutes or until softened.
  5. Season the migas with salt to taste.
  6. Push the migas to the sides of the pan and crack the eggs into the center. Cook for 3-4 minutes or until egg whites are set.
  7. Stir the migas to combine with the eggs. Serve immediately.
